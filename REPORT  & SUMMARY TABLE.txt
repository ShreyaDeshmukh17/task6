!!Final Report Summary!!

Observation:
Passwords using only lowercase or uppercase letters are extremely easy to crack (1 day).
The inclusion of numbers and symbols increases password strength exponentially, with cracking time rising from days to millions or sextillions of years.

Conclusion:
A strong password should be at least 14â€“16 characters long and include:

Uppercase + lowercase letters
Numbers
Special characters/symbols
Unpredictable structure

Best Practices Learned:

Never reuse passwords across sites.
Use a password manager to store complex passwords.
Avoid personal names or dictionary words.
Change passwords periodically and especially after security breaches.
